How to Calculate BMR

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions, such as breathing, circulation, and cell production, while at rest. The formula to calculate BMR is different for men and women:

For Men:

BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 * weight in kg) + (4.799 * height in cm) - (5.677 * age in years)

For Women:

BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 * weight in kg) + (3.098 * height in cm) - (4.330 * age in years)

For example, if a man weighs 70 kg, is 175 cm tall, and is 25 years old, his BMR would be calculated as follows:

BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 * 70) + (4.799 * 175) - (5.677 * 25) = 1,679.65 calories/day

Similarly, if a woman weighs 60 kg, is 165 cm tall, and is 30 years old, her BMR would be calculated as follows:

BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 * 60) + (3.098 * 165) - (4.330 * 30) = 1,383.38 calories/day
